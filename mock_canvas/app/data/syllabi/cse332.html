Washington University in St. Louis · Summer 2026
Instructor: Dr. A. Whitman · Lecture: Mon/Wed 10:00–11:20, Whitaker 218 · Lab: Fri 13:00–14:50, Urbauer 215

Course Description

CSE 332 builds on introductory programming to develop the design skills needed for larger software systems. We study object-oriented modeling, inheritance and polymorphism, generics, the Java collections framework, common design patterns, and an introduction to concurrency. The course is project-driven: most weeks include a lab on Friday and a homework that applies the week's concepts.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the term you should be able to decompose a problem into collaborating objects, apply appropriate design patterns, reason about generic and concurrent code, and justify your design decisions in writing.

Grading

Homework and labs together account for 45% of your grade, the term project for 30%, the midterm for 10%, and the final exam for 15%. Late work loses 10% per day and is not accepted more than three days late without a documented excuse.

Schedule & Key Dates

The pace is brisk because this is a summer offering. The midterm will be held in class on the Wednesday of week 7, and it covers everything through generics. Project proposals are due midway through the term as Milestone 1, with a working prototype expected a few weeks later. The final project demo is scheduled for the Friday of week 14, during which each team will present a live walkthrough of their system to the staff; there are no extensions on the demo because grades are due to the registrar the following Monday.

Academic Integrity

You may discuss ideas with classmates, but all submitted code must be your own unless an assignment explicitly permits collaboration. Violations are reported to the Office of the Provost in accordance with university policy.
